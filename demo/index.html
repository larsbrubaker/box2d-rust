Deterministic math live wasm

The polygons below are rotated with the ported b2MakeRot / b2TransformPoint, using Box2D's hand-rolled deterministic cosine/sine — running in your browser as WebAssembly compiled from the Rust port.

Geometry queries live wasm

Move your mouse over the canvas. A ray from the left edge tracks the cursor and is cast against every shape with the ported b2RayCastPolygon / b2RayCastCircle / b2RayCastCapsule / b2RayCastSegment — the pentagon comes from b2ComputeHull. The probe triangle follows the cursor and reports GJK closest points from b2ShapeDistance.

Contact manifolds live wasm

Drag your mouse to move the rotating shape against the fixed box. Contact points and the manifold normal come from the ported b2CollidePolygons / b2CollidePolygonAndCircle / b2CollidePolygonAndCapsule. Red points are penetrating (negative separation); green are speculative contacts. Click the canvas to cycle the shape.

Falling bodies live wasm

A full simulation driven by the ported b2World_Step: broad-phase pairs, narrow-phase manifolds, graph-colored soft-constraint solving with sub-stepping, restitution, and island sleeping. Click anywhere to drop more boxes and balls.

Stacking live wasm

A box pyramid solved by the ported engine. Watch the island fall asleep once it settles (the readout shows the awake body count reaching zero). Click to drop a heavy ball on it and wake everything back up.
